# AI in the UK: Session Overview

## Read This First

- You are building a realistic AI proposal for a real UK sector problem.
- You will give three short contributions: `first pitch`, `anti-pitch`, `final pitch`.
- Each pitch is 1 minute.
- Strong teams are clear, realistic, and honest about risk.

## Your Task

Your group is assigned one UK sector. Build a proposal that is useful, realistic, and socially responsible.

Answer these questions clearly:

- What is the problem?
- Why does it matter now?
- How exactly could AI help?
- Who benefits, and who might be harmed?
- What risks or ethical concerns could appear?
- What safeguards would reduce those risks?

## Group Roles (Pick 1 each)

### AI Operator
Uses AI tools to generate, test, and improve ideas.

### Researcher
Checks assumptions, finds evidence, and pressure-tests weak points.

### Scribe
Keeps a clear written record of ideas and the final pitch.

### Orator
Delivers the spoken pitches.

## Pitch Templates

### First Pitch (1 minute)

1. Sector
2. Problem
3. AI solution
4. Expected benefits

### Anti-Pitch (1 minute)

You critique another team's proposal.

1. Key risks
2. Ethical concerns
3. Moral concerns
4. Likely unintended harms

### Final Pitch (1 minute)

Return to your original proposal and improve it after critique.

1. Main concerns raised
2. How you will mitigate risks
3. Why your proposal is still strong and responsible

## Workshop Flow (75 minutes total)

1. Introduction from Facilitator (7 min)   
2. Research and first pitch preparation (15 min)  
3. First pitch round (8 min)  
4. Research and anti-pitch preparation (10 min)
5. Anti-pitch round (8 min)  
6. Final revision (10 min)  
7. Final pitch round (8 min)  
8. Vote and Feedback (10 min)
9. Award to best AI solution (2 min)


## What Makes a Strong Solution?

The best ideas:

- address a real and important problem
- use AI in a meaningful, realistic way
- show awareness of risk and ethics
- include practical safeguards
- are clear, convincing, and well argued

## Ground Rules

- Focus on real problems, not sci-fi ideas.
- Critique constructively.
- Consider fairness, sustainability, and unintended harm.
- Use AI critically, not blindly.
